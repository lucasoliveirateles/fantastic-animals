Fantastic Animals

Fox

Foxes are small to medium-sized omnivorous mammals belonging to several genera of the Canidae family.

Twelve species belong to the monophyletic group of "true foxes" of the genus Vulpes.

Foxes live on every continent except Antarctica.

Squirrel

Squirrels are found all over the world, mostly in temperate or tropical climate zones, but also in some cold climate zones.

In Brazil, they are also known as serelepe, caxinguelê, caxinxe, quatimirim, quatipuru, agutipuru or acutipuru.

In Galicia and in some parts of Portugal, it is also known as skiing.

Bear

Bears are a family of plantigrade mammals of the order Carnivora, usually large, containing bears and pandas.

Some common characteristics of bears are thick coat, short tail, developed nose, and non-retractable claws.

Ursids are generally omnivorous animals, but they feed mainly on meat.

Bears exist on all continents, with the exception of Africa, although some sources claim to have seen the nandi bear, but without proving its existence.

Wolves

The wolf is a species of canid mammal in the genus Canis, in the family of dogs and foxes.

It is an Ice Age survivor, originating from the Late Pleistocene, about 300,000 years ago.

Gray wolves are typically apex predators in the ecosystems they occupy.

Although they are not as adaptable to human presence as is usually the case with other species of canids wolves developed in diverse environments such as temperate forests, deserts, mountains, tundra, taigas, grasslands and even in some urban areas.

Macaco

Monkey is a term of African origin used as a common designation for all species of apes or anthropoid primates.

It is strictly applied in Brazil to cebidae in general. In the strict sense, "monkey" refers to primate species belonging to the genus Macaca.

The name tamarin originates from the continental Caribbean.

It is widely used in Brazil, where it usually applies to species of the genus Cebus, in the South, and to small species, or marmosets, in the North.

Lion

Lion is a species of carnivorous mammal of the genus Panthera and family Felidae.

The species is currently found in sub-Saharan Africa and Asia, with a single remaining endangered population, in the Gir Forest National Park, Gujarat, India.

It went extinct in North Africa and Southwest Asia in historical times, and until the Late Pleistocene, some 10,000 years ago, it was the most widespread large terrestrial mammal after humans, being found in most of Africa, in much of Eurasia, from Western Europe to India, and in America, from the Yukon to Mexico.

It is one of the four big cats, with some males exceeding 250 kilograms in weight, being the second largest recent cat after the tiger.
